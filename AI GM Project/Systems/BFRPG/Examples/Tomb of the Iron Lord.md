### **\[MODULE: THE TOMB OF THE IRON LORD\]**

**Rank/Level:** Levels 1 (Designed for a 3-4 person party)

**Location:** A ruined barrow mound deep in the Whispering Woods.

**The Hook:** A local village elder has pooled together 50 gp to hire mercenaries. Goblins have moved into the ancient barrow mound just outside of town. They’ve been stealing livestock and ambushing travelers, and the villagers want them routed before they amass a larger warband.

#### ---

**ROOM 1: The Guardian (The Lookout Post)**

* **Narrative:** The heavy stone doors of the tomb have been cracked open. The air is damp and smells of wet fur. Just inside the entrance, two bored goblins sit around a sputtering campfire, arguing over a stolen, half-eaten chicken.  
* **Mechanics:** 2x Goblins. Distracted sentries.  
* **Engine Directive:** Require Jax to roll his Move Silently (25%) to scout ahead. If the party just kicks the door in, the GM must roll a 2d6 Reaction Roll. If the result is 6 or lower, one goblin fights while the other attempts to flee to Room 4 to warn the boss.

#### **ROOM 2: The Puzzle/Trap (The Corridor of Faces)**

* **Narrative:** A long, dusty corridor lined with the carved stone faces of ancient warriors. The floor tiles look slightly uneven, and there are tiny, dark holes drilled into the open mouths of the stone faces.  
* **Mechanics:** Poison Dart Trap.  
* **Engine Directive:** The GM must ask the players for their "Marching Order." Jax can attempt a Find/Remove Traps (20%) roll. If the trap is triggered by the front player stepping on a pressure plate, the target must Save vs. Death Ray or Poison. If they fail, a dart strikes them for 1d4 damage and they are nauseous (-1 to Attack Rolls) for the rest of the dungeon.

#### **ROOM 3: The Setback (The Collapsed Burial Chamber)**

* **Narrative:** A side chamber where the ceiling has caved in. Amidst the rubble is a glint of silver from a dropped coin purse. However, the ceiling groans ominously, and something long, multi-legged, and pale is skittering through the rocks.  
* **Mechanics:** 1x Giant Centipede and an unstable environment.  
* **Engine Directive:** If the players try to grab the coin purse, the Centipede drops from the ceiling. **OSR Combat Rule:** The Centipede's bite requires a Save vs. Death Ray or Poison. If failed, the player becomes violently ill (cannot move faster than a walk, cannot attack) for 10 days.

#### **ROOM 4: The Climax (The Warlord's Rest)**

* **Narrative:** The central burial chamber. A massive, sealed stone sarcophagus sits in the center. A bloated Goblin Boss, wearing pieces of rusted, oversized ceremonial armor, barks orders at three normal Goblins who are fruitlessly trying to pry the sarcophagus open with crowbars.  
* **Mechanics:** 1x Goblin Boss, 3x Goblins.  
* **Engine Directive:** The Boss has 2 Hit Dice (HD) and hits harder than the others. **Morale Check Trigger:** If Elara casts *Sleep* and knocks out the minions, OR if the party focuses down and kills the Boss first, the GM MUST force a Morale Check (Target 7\) on any remaining conscious goblins. If they fail, they drop their weapons and surrender or flee.

#### **ROOM 5: The Reward (The Warlord's Hoard)**

* **Narrative:** With the goblins defeated, Kaelen's massive 16 Strength is enough to slide the heavy stone lid off the sarcophagus. Inside lie the dusty bones of the Iron Lord, completely untouched by the goblin scavengers.  
* **Mechanics:** Classic Loot & Progression.  
* **Engine Directive:** Award the party the village bounty (50 gp). Inside the tomb, they find an additional 150 gp in ancient silver coins, a fine Masterwork Shortsword (+1 to Attack Rolls, but normal damage) perfect for Jax, and a sealed bone scroll tube containing a random Level 1 Magic-User Spell for Elara to copy into her spellbook.

\* **\*\*Core Stats:\*\*** Triggered by weight on specific floor tiles.
